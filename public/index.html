How to dispute a medical bill

Medical billing errors are shockingly common — studies show up to 80% of hospital bills contain mistakes. Common errors include duplicate charges for the same service, upcoding (billing for a more complex procedure than was performed), separate charges for items that should be bundled into a facility fee, and balance billing errors where you're charged more than your insurance's allowed amount.

To dispute a medical bill, you need an itemized statement listing every charge by CPT code and amount. BillFighter's AI analyzes your bill line by line, identifies which charges are potentially erroneous, and writes a formal dispute letter you can send directly to the hospital's billing department. Most hospitals respond within 30 days and many remove or reduce disputed charges.

Common medical billing errors we find: duplicate ER visit charges · upcoded procedure codes · $20–$50 charges for single pills (ibuprofen, Tylenol) · facility fees that duplicate room charges · unbundled lab tests · contrast material charged without imaging.
